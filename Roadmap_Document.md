## SHEET 3: ROADMAP NOTES

**Author:** Shristi Sharmistha
**Last updated:** June 28, 2025

---

**Why we built in this order**

Q1 was entirely about building the right foundation. We made a deliberate
decision not to build any marketing or growth features in Q1. The
reasoning was simple — a product that retains users poorly will not
benefit from more users. Get the core experience right first.

Custom food was not in the original Q1 scope. We added it after
discovering during USDA integration that Indian foods like chapati,
dal, and paneer were largely absent from the 300,000-food USDA database.
This was an unplanned discovery we fast-tracked because it directly
threatened whether Indian users could trust Nutrivana's data accuracy.
This was the right call. Custom food is now our strongest retention
driver — 2.3x Day 30 retention for users who create at least 1
custom food.

Q2 was about proving the product works with real users. The beta program
came before public launch deliberately — to catch UX problems with 200
users instead of 3000. The bar graph redesign is the best example of
why beta programs matter. 12 out of 40 beta users found the analysis
confusing. Fixing it before public launch turned those frustrated beta
users into our best App Store reviewers — and drove NPS from 34 to 41.

---

**The pregnant women discovery**

We did not build Nutrivana for pregnant women. We built it for
general health-conscious Indians aged 22-45. The pregnant women
segment emerged from the data.

In June user research, 5 pregnant women beta users revealed they are
3.1x more engaged than the average user. They use micronutrient
tracking 4x more than other users. They have specific doctor-recommended
targets (folate 600mcg, iron 27mg, calcium 1000mg in trimester-specific
doses) that no other app supports at this precision level. And they
refer other pregnant women — the segment is self-propagating.

This discovery changes Q3 completely. The two pregnant women features
(prenatal presets and supplement enhancement) are now Q3 priority 1
and 2 — above custom recipes and barcode scanner which were
originally planned first.

---

**What we deliberately did NOT build**

Social sharing — our target user tracks nutrition for personal health
goals not social validation. Adding social features risks making
Nutrivana feel like a fitness performance app. May reconsider Q4
if user research changes this view.

Photo-based calorie scanning — accuracy not good enough for a health
app. A 20% error rate in calorie estimation destroys the trust we
built through accurate EER formulas and USDA data. Will not build
until accuracy can be validated to within 10%.

Meal planning — premature. Users need to build a logging habit
before they will engage with planning. Meal planning is for users
with a 30-day logging streak. We do not yet have enough users
at that stage.

---

**Q3 confidence levels explained**

High confidence features (Simplified Onboarding, Prenatal Presets,
Custom Recipe Builder) have clear evidence from Q2 data:
- 38% goal setting drop-off is a proven blocker
- Pregnant women 3.1x engagement is a proven segment
- Custom recipes is the top NPS open-text request

Medium confidence features (Supplement Enhancement, Barcode Scanner)
have clear user need but implementation complexity is less certain.
Scope may change during Q3 sprint planning.

Low confidence features (AI Recommendations, Offline Support) are
aspirational. AI recommendations requires training data we do not
yet have at sufficient volume. Offline support is technically complex
(service workers, local-first architecture, conflict resolution).
Both may move to Q4.

---

**How this roadmap connects to OKRs**

Every feature on this roadmap maps to at least one OKR key result.
If a feature cannot be connected to an OKR KR it does not go on the
roadmap — it goes on a wishlist for future quarters.

Q1 features → OKR Q1 Objective 1 (accuracy and trust) and
Objective 3 (ship MVP proudly)

Q2 features → OKR Q2 Objective 1 (habit formation) and
Objective 2 (Indian users coming back)

Q3 features → OKR Q3 (to be defined in July planning session)
with Day 7 retention 55% and pregnant women segment as
the two north star objectives
